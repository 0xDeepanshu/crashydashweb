# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences arriving primarily from Twitter/X links:

- **Crypto-native gamers** who already hold wallets and understand on-chain mechanics. They want to know what makes Crashy Dash different: MPP payments, MagicBlocks privacy, AI agent racing.
- **Crypto-curious newcomers** who've never connected a wallet. They need to feel that playing is simple, safe, and fun before they click through to the Twitter Player Card.

Both arrive in a fast-scroll, short-attention context (Twitter feed → landing page → back to Twitter to play). The page has seconds, not minutes, to land its message.

## Product Purpose

Crashy Dash is a blockchain-powered voxel racing game that runs entirely inside a Twitter Player Card. Players connect their wallet, pick their ride, and race directly within the X/Twitter interface.

This landing page exists to:
1. Explain what Crashy Dash is and why it's different (MPP, MagicBlocks, AI agents)
2. Build excitement through scroll-driven storytelling and animation
3. Drive visitors back to Twitter/X to start playing via the embedded Player Card

Success: visitor clicks through to Twitter and starts playing.

## Brand Personality

**Playful, friendly, fast.**

Crashy Dash doesn't take itself too seriously. It's a racing game, not a financial instrument. The tone is energetic and inviting, never intimidating or overly technical. Crypto complexity is hidden behind simple language. The interface should feel like the first 10 seconds of a great arcade game: immediate, exciting, and impossible to walk away from.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS landing pages.** No hero-metrics templates, no identical card grids, no corporate blue gradients, no "trusted by 500+ companies" social proof strips. This is a game, not enterprise software.
- **Overly complex DeFi dashboards.** No yield tables, no tokenomics charts front-and-center. The blockchain layer is a feature, not the headline.
- **Template-feeling crypto sites.** No cookie-cutter "neon on black with hexagons" that every web3 project uses. Personality over pattern.

## Design Principles

1. **Story over structure.** The page is a scroll-driven narrative, not a feature catalog. Each section reveals the next chapter: what it is, how it works, why it matters, go play.
2. **Simplicity is the superpower.** If a crypto newcomer can't understand a section in 3 seconds, it's too complex. Hide technical depth behind approachable language and visuals.
3. **Motion earns attention.** Animation isn't decoration; it's the storytelling medium. Every scroll-triggered reveal should feel intentional and rewarding, guiding the eye through the narrative.
4. **Fun is non-negotiable.** Every element should feel like it belongs in a game. Hover states should be playful. Transitions should feel fast and alive. Nothing static, nothing boring.
5. **One exit, one action.** The page has one job: get visitors to Twitter to play. Every section should build toward that single CTA. No distractions, no competing links.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA as baseline for color contrast, especially where text overlays animated backgrounds or video frames.
- Reduced motion support via `prefers-reduced-motion`: the scroll-driven storytelling must remain comprehensible with animations disabled (progressive enhancement, not motion-dependent content).
- The mixed audience includes people unfamiliar with crypto jargon; UX copy must be jargon-free or explain terms inline.
